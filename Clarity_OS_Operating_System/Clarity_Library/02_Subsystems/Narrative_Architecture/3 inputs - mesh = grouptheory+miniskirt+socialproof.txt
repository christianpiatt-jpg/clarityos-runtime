B. Group theory (mathematics)
- Core definition: mathematical study of groups (sets with binary operation satisfying closure, associativity, identity, inverses).
- Main classes & concepts: permutation groups, matrix (linear) groups, transformation groups, abstract groups, finite groups, Lie groups, topological groups, algebraic groups.
- Key historical roots: Euler, Gauss, Lagrange, Ruffini, Abel, Évariste Galois, Cayley, Cauchy, Felix Klein, Sophus Lie.
- Principal results/objects: classification of finite simple groups; group presentations (generators & relations); Cayley graphs; group actions; representation theory; conjugacy, normal subgroups, quotient groups.
- Branches & subfields: finite group theory, representation theory, Lie theory, combinatorial/geometric group theory, algebraic groups, profinite groups.
- Applications: physics (symmetry, conservation laws, Standard Model), chemistry/materials (molecular symmetry, point/space groups), cryptography (elliptic curves, Diffie–Hellman), algebraic topology (fundamental group), algebraic number theory (class groups), harmonic analysis.
- Methods & problems: word problem, isomorphism problem, classification, group cohomology, Cayley graphs, Milnor–Švarc quasi‑isometry.
- Examples/illustrations: Rubik’s Cube group, symmetric group Sn, alternating group An, Z (integers), torus example, Weyl groups.
- Tools/terminology: representation ρ: G → GL(V), Haar measure, Maschke’s theorem, Schur’s lemma.

C. Social proof (informational social influence)
- Definition: tendency to copy others’ actions/decisions in ambiguous situations; coined/popularized by Robert Cialdini.
- Mechanisms: uncertainty about correct behavior; perceived knowledgeability of others; similarity to group (in‑group effects); task importance; cultural moderating effects (collectivist vs individualist).
- Classic studies: Muzafer Sherif (autokinetic effect, 1935); Asch/Ashen (conformity line studies not identical but related); eyewitness conformity experiments; door‑to‑door donor list experiment.
- Related phenomena: information cascades, herd behavior, bandwagon effect, bystander effect, copycat suicides (Werther effect), normative social influence (contrast).
- Domains of application: marketing/e‑commerce (testimonials, reviews), social media (followers, likes, views), entertainment (planted applause, laugh tracks), public health/messaging, politics, crowd behavior.
- Moderators & caveats: cultural differences, perceived similarity, task importance, private acceptance vs public compliance, risk of premature convergence on poor choices.
- Modern research/tools: agent‑based models for copycat suicide; recommender systems influence; dual‑process models for algorithmic recommendation influence.

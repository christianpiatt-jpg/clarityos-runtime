# Physical Vectors — Core Model
**Version:** v1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-22

Physical vectors are the macro-scale movement systems that determine how civilizations form, interact, and propagate. Each vector has direction, strength, seasonality, and historical impact.

---

## 1. Jet Streams
High-altitude atmospheric rivers that shape:
- climate patterns
- agricultural cycles
- migration corridors
- trade winds
- conflict and cooperation zones

Jet streams influence where civilizations can sustain large populations and how quickly ideas travel.

---

## 2. Ocean Currents
Large-scale water movements that determine:
- maritime trade routes
- cultural exchange
- disease spread
- colonization patterns
- basin connectivity

Ocean currents are the backbone of global economic and cultural flow.

---

## 3. Monsoon Systems
Seasonal wind and rain cycles that shape:
- agricultural reliability
- population density
- ritual calendars
- economic rhythms
- political stability

Monsoons create predictable cycles that anchor ritual-heavy civilizations.

---

## 4. Steppe Routes
Overland corridors across grasslands and plains that enable:
- nomadic expansion
- empire formation
- military mobility
- cultural hybridization

Steppe routes explain the rise of the Mongols, Turks, and other mobile civilizations.

---

## 5. Maritime Corridors
Navigable sea lanes that support:
- trade
- piracy
- cultural blending
- creolization
- technological diffusion

Maritime corridors are the physical substrate for hybrid cultures like the Caribbean and Swahili Coast.

---

## Structural Notes
- Physical vectors are slow-changing and form the “terrain physics” of civilization.
- All higher modules (basins, languages, digital currents) sit on top of these vectors.
- Forecasting depends heavily on vector stability and shifts.

Hydronic geometry (flow/pressure model)
- Conduits: edges represent information flow or causal flow (pipe segments)
  - Thick conduit = multiple corroborating docs/observations
  - Thin conduit = single or weak documentation
- Reservoirs: repositories of evidence or institutional pressure
  - EHR Reservoir = AL (EHR Audit Log) + IT node (critical evidence)
  - Procedural Reservoir = RD + HR + procedural flags
  - Medical/Clinical Reservoir = OE + clinical notes (sensitive)
- Pumps: actors that add pressure/force into the system
  - Z pump (Zimmer’s reports/communications)  
  - OGC pump (legal framing/access events)  
  - PA pump (Plaintiff’s protected activity raises upstream pressure)
- Valves: decision points that can open/close flow (policy, procedural steps)
  - Notice valve (did plaintiff receive notice?)  
  - Proposal/Decision valve (separation of roles?)  
  - Access-control valve (EHR access authorization)
- Leaks: contradictions, missing documentation, and breaches
  - Witness contradiction leaks: Z ↔ L ↔ M contradictions drain the credibility pressure.  
  - Medical‑boundary leak: OE/AL discrepancies create a leak exposing OGC access.  
  - Timeline leak: out‑of‑order timestamps create pressure loss in the Agency narrative.
- Turbulence / vortices: intense local conflict areas where small changes cascade
  - Teams Recording vortex (T): conflicting recollections create chaotic local flow.  
  - Care Disruption vortex (CT + CD): loss of care amplifies downstream damage (constructive discharge).
- Backflow / contamination: improper use of medical info contaminating decision flow

⭐ Signal Extraction (Geopolitical + Pressure Geometry)
1. Single‑Point Fragility
A fire at one aluminum facility → national‑level bottleneck.
This is classic single‑node dependency: when one upstream node fails, the entire downstream system absorbs the shock.

2. Pressure Stacking
Two loads collide:

Policy load (tariffs)

Physical load (factory outage)

When loads stack faster than systems can adapt, fragility surfaces.

3. Efficiency vs. Resilience
Automotive supply chains were optimized for:

cost

speed

just‑in‑time flow

But optimization reduces slack.
Slack is what absorbs shocks.
Remove slack → shocks propagate.

4. Institutional Lag
Industry requests relief → policy response lags.
This creates:

temporal mismatch

uncertainty

operational drag

Lag is a form of pressure accumulation.

5. System‑Wide Exposure
A localized disruption becomes:

margin compression

production delays

pricing pressure

strategic vulnerability

This is the geometry of over‑concentration.

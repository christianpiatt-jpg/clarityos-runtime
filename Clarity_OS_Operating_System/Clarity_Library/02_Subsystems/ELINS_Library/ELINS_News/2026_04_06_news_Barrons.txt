Tesla stock slid after first-quarter deliveries missed expectations, prompting analysts including Goldman Sachs and Baird to lower price targets. (AFP via Getty Images)
Key Points

About This Summary

Tesla’s first-quarter deliveries of 358,023 vehicles missed Wall Street expectations, causing its stock to drop 5.4%.

Tesla produced 50,000 more cars than sold, increasing inventory, and battery storage deployment fell to 8.8 GWh.

Analysts cut price targets following the report, with investors now focused on upcoming AI initiatives and Q1 earnings.

Investors didn’t like Tesla’s first-quarter delivery numbers. Neither did Wall Street.

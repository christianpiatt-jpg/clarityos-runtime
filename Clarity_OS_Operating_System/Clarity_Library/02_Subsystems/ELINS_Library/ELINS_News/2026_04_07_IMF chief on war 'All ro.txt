The head of the International Monetary Fund warns that the war in Iran will drive higher inflation and weaker growth, even if hostilities end shortly. Speaking to Reuters ahead of the IMF's global forecast due later this week, Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva said the economic forces already unleashed will be hard to reverse. "All roads now lead to higher prices and slower growth," she said. With a U.S. deadline for Iran to reopen the vital Strait of Hormuz looming Tuesday night, the price of benchmark Brent climbed to $111 a barrel.

The market isn’t calm right now… it’s conflicted. And that’s actually more dangerous.

 Because underneath the surface, three forces are pulling in completely different directions:
 • Oil is surging
 • Inflation is trying to come back
 • The Fed is losing flexibility

 Most people are looking at the market going, “Stocks are holding up… things must be fine.”

 I don’t see it that way.
 ⸻

Here’s what’s really happening:

 We are quietly entering a second-wave inflation risk cycle. But this time, it’s not driven by consumer demand… It’s being driven by supply shocks; specifically, energy.

 When oil spikes, it doesn’t stay isolated. It moves through the entire system:
 Energy → Transportation → Goods → Consumer prices → Corporate margins

 And we’re already seeing early signs of that chain reaction.

 ⸻
At the same time…

 The Fed is stuck. Strong labor data means they can’t cut rates quickly. But rising energy costs mean inflation could start climbing again. That’s a problem.
• Strong jobs data is pushing rate cuts further out (now expected late 2026) 
 • Markets briefly priced in rate hikes again (~45% probability) 
 
Fed is stuck between:
 • Slowing growth
 • Rising inflation

 Now the Fed isn’t leading the market anymore…The market is forcing the Fed to react.
 ⸻

And here’s the part most people are missing:

 Right now, we have:
 • Stocks holding up today on ceasefire hopes
 • Crypto rallying (Bitcoin near $70K)
 • Gold and yields rising at the same time (rare signal)

 That combination doesn’t normally happen, but it matters:
 • Rising gold = fear hedge
 • Rising yields = inflation concern
 • Rising stocks = optimism

 It tells you one thing: Risk is building… but it hasn’t been fully priced in yet.
 ⸻

Why this matters:

 When gas crosses ~$4:
 • Consumers pull back
 • Spending slows
 • Earnings compress
 • Valuations follow

 We’ve seen this pattern before. Not every time leads to a major drawdown… But it always shifts the risk profile of the market.

Historically:
 • Markets have seen ~30–40% drawdowns during similar setups 

That doesn’t mean it happens again… But it raises the probability distribution of downside.
 ⸻

My read: This isn’t a crash setup today. But it is a fragile equilibrium.

 We’re transitioning from:
 A liquidity-driven market
 To a risk-driven market

 And in that kind of environment:
 • Balance sheets matter
 • Cash flow matters
 • Discipline matters

 A lot more than momentum.

What I’m Watching This Week:
 • CPI (inflation confirmation or escalation)
 • Fed minutes (policy tone shift)
 • Oil trajectory (does $110 hold or spike?)
 • Earnings (Delta = consumer + travel health signal)
 ⸻

The next move in this market won’t be driven by hype…It will be driven by one question:

Is inflation about to re-accelerate again? Because if it is everything gets repriced.

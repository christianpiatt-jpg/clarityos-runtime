\# Global Arcs

\## Dewey: 610



\## Summary

Global arcs are long-range atmospheric patterns that shape collective behavior, institutional motion, and cultural drift. They operate on months-to-decades timescales.



\## Core Concepts

\- Long-range patterns

\- Collective drift

\- Atmospheric motion



\## Body

Global arcs describe the large-scale movements of pressure across populations and institutions. They reveal how collective narratives rise, peak, and collapse. Operators use global arcs to anticipate turning points, identify instability, and prepare for systemic shifts.

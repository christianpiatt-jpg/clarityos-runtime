\# Generational Weather

\## Dewey: 620



\## Summary

Generational weather describes the atmospheric patterns that emerge from generational cohorts. Each cohort produces predictable pressure signatures and narrative tendencies.



\## Core Concepts

\- Cohort signatures

\- Generational fronts

\- Narrative cycles



\## Body

Generational weather maps the pressure patterns produced by different age cohorts. Each generation carries distinct emotional physics, narrative tendencies, and stability thresholds. These patterns interact to create generational fronts—zones of friction, acceleration, or collapse. Operators use generational weather to understand long-term cultural motion.

# PENTAGON→SPHERE GEOMETRY — OPERATOR NOTES
Version: v2
Layer: System Mapping → Multi‑Scale Geometry

------------------------------------------------------------
## PURPOSE
PENTAGON→SPHERE GEOMETRY maps the system across multiple scales and
translates complex relational environments into a stable geometric model.

Its core function:
→ Identify center‑of‑mass, load paths, stress points, and relief vectors.

This module allows the operator to “see the whole system at once” without
overwhelm or narrative distortion.

It is the macro‑geometry engine of ClarityOS v2.

------------------------------------------------------------
## INPUTS
- G.output (geometric forms)
- PATTERNED_SHAPES.output (relational patterns)
- GLOBAL_OS signals (macro‑scale context)
- Environmental or institutional load (optional)

------------------------------------------------------------
## OUTPUTS
- center_of_mass  
- load_paths  
- stress_points  
- relief_vectors  
- multi‑scale system geometry  

These outputs feed into:
- MARKOV_QC (temporal weighting)
- Executive layers (v1)
- Meta‑Executive (oversight)

------------------------------------------------------------
## OPERATOR POSTURE
When using this module, the operator maintains:

- altitude (zoomed‑out awareness)
- non‑urgency
- structural curiosity
- patience with complexity
- comfort with multi‑scale geometry

The operator asks:
“What is the shape of the system as a whole?”

Not:
“What is happening to me right now?”

------------------------------------------------------------
## FAILURE MODES
This module becomes distorted when:

- the operator zooms in too far (losing macro‑geometry)
- emotional charge collapses the system into a personal story
- the operator overfits patterns
- the system is overloaded with too many variables
- the operator tries to “solve” instead of “map”

Symptoms include:
- tunnel vision
- misidentifying stress points
- confusing local turbulence with global instability
- false urgency
- misreading load paths

------------------------------------------------------------
## RECOVERY PROTOCOL
If the mapping becomes noisy:

1. Zoom out.  
2. Remove all personal meaning.  
3. Identify only:
   - center‑of‑mass
   - load paths
   - stress points
   - relief vectors
4. Re‑run the geometry from a higher altitude.  
5. Confirm the system shape before passing to MARKOV_QC.  

This restores macro‑clarity.

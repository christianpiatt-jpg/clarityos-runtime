# IDENTITY LAYER v2 — OPERATOR NOTES
Version: v2
Layer: Self Geometry → Stabilized Identity

------------------------------------------------------------
## PURPOSE
IDENTITY_LAYER_V2 stabilizes the operator’s sense of self by grounding it
in geometry rather than narrative.

Its core function:
→ Produce a clear, nonreactive, structurally stable reference frame.

This layer ensures that identity is:
- downstream of interpretation
- insulated from emotional turbulence
- anchored in structure, not story
- stable under pressure

It is the “center of gravity” of ClarityOS v2.

------------------------------------------------------------
## INPUTS
- QC_ENGINE.output (probabilities, thresholds, force-weights)
- Environmental context (optional)
- System load (optional)

------------------------------------------------------------
## OUTPUTS
- clear_self  
- nonreactive_posture  
- stable_reference_frame  
- identity-neutral interpretation  

These outputs feed directly into:
- AGENCY_ENGINE_V2  
- MARKOV_QC  
- Executive layers (v1) indirectly  

------------------------------------------------------------
## OPERATOR POSTURE
When using this module, the operator maintains:

- calm neutrality  
- grounded presence  
- slow identity movement  
- separation of self from signal  
- structural awareness  

The operator asks:
“What is *me* and what is *the environment*?”

Not:
“What does this say about who I am?”

------------------------------------------------------------
## FAILURE MODES
Identity becomes unstable when:

- QC is bypassed  
- emotional charge is high  
- the operator personalizes external patterns  
- narrative overrides geometry  
- the system is overloaded  

Symptoms include:
- identity wobble  
- reactivity  
- over-identification  
- collapsing self into the situation  
- agency distortion  

------------------------------------------------------------
## RECOVERY PROTOCOL
If identity destabilizes:

1. Pause all interpretation.  
2. Return to QC probabilities and thresholds.  
3. Remove all personal meaning.  
4. Re-anchor in geometric self-description:
   - “Where am I?”
   - “What is my posture?”
   - “What is the shape of my role here?”
5. Re-establish the stable reference frame.  

This restores identity clarity.

\# Collapse Mechanics

\## Dewey: 120



\## Summary

Collapse mechanics describe how systems fail under pressure. Collapse is predictable, structured, and follows identifiable sequences.



\## Core Concepts

\- Collapse sequence

\- Threshold breach

\- Acceleration spike



\## Body

Collapse occurs when pressure exceeds the stability envelope. The sequence begins with curvature, followed by drift, then acceleration, and finally jerk. Collapse is not random; it follows a mechanical progression. Operators intervene early by reducing gradients and restoring boundary integrity.

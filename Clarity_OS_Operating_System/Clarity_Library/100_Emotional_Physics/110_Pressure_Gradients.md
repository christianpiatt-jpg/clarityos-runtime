\# Pressure Gradients

\## Dewey: 110



\## Summary

Pressure gradients describe how emotional force distributes across rings and boundaries. They determine where collapse begins and how fast it propagates.



\## Core Concepts

\- Pressure slope

\- Load concentration

\- Collapse vectors



\## Body

A pressure gradient is the difference in emotional load between two points in a system. Steep gradients create instability and accelerate collapse. Shallow gradients allow for controlled dissipation. Operators track gradients to predict where a system will fail and how to stabilize it.

\# Operator Sensing

\## Dewey: 160



\## Summary

Operator sensing is the skill of detecting pressure, gradients, and collapse signals in real time. It is the operator’s primary diagnostic tool.



\## Core Concepts

\- Sensory bandwidth

\- Signal detection

\- Early warning cues



\## Body

Operators sense pressure through emotional, physical, and narrative cues. High-bandwidth sensing allows early detection of collapse sequences. Low-bandwidth sensing leads to late intervention and greater instability. Training increases sensitivity and reduces noise.

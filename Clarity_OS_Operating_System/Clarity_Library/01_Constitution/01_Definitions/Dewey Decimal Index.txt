## 000 — Clarity OS (System‑Level)

**000.0 — Clarity OS**  
The parent constitutional operating system governing structural integrity, drift detection, predictive correction, and subsystem inheritance.

**000.1 — Constitutional Layer**  
The set of invariant rules, boundaries, and principles that apply across all Clarity OS subsystems.

**000.2 — Invariants**  
System properties that must remain stable regardless of domain (e.g., boundary enforcement, diagnostic precedence).

**000.3 — System Hierarchy Rule**  
Interpretation priority:  
Constitutional Layer → Diagnostic Engines → Subsystems → Modules.

**000.4 — Subsystem Inheritance Rule**  
All subsystems inherit the constitutional layer and diagnostic engines from Clarity OS.

***

## 100 — Diagnostic Architecture (Shared Engines)

**100.0 — Diagnostic Engines**  
Shared mechanisms that detect, classify, and evaluate drift or misalignment across systems.

**100.1 — Drift Detection**  
The identification of deviation from constitutional or structural integrity.

**100.2 — Structural Misalignment**  
A condition in which authority, process, communication, or culture diverges from constitutional rules.

**100.3 — Boundary Enforcement**  
Detection and correction of authority, role, or scope violations.

**100.4 — Corrective Propagation**  
System‑generated pathways that restore integrity after drift is detected.

**100.5 — Diagnostic Precedence Rule**  
Detection precedes correction; correction must reference the originating diagnostic signal.

***

## 200 — Subsystems (Domain Implementations)

**200.0 — Subsystem**  
A domain‑specific implementation of Clarity OS that inherits core invariants and diagnostics.

**200.1 — Lawbridg**  
Legal operating system applying Clarity OS to litigation, investigation, procedure, and evidentiary integrity.

**200.2 — Salesbridg**  
Intercultural sales operating system applying Clarity OS to persuasion, communication, and relational boundaries.

**200.3 — Businessbridg**  
Organizational and operational integrity operating system applying Clarity OS to institutions and workflows.

**200.4 — Domain Adaptation**  
Customization of modules and vocabulary without altering constitutional invariants.

## 300 — Modules (Functional Units)

**300.0 — Module**  
A functional detection unit within a subsystem.

**300.1 — Module Family**  
A related set of modules operating together inside a subsystem.

**300.2 — Operational‑Drift Scanner**

**300.3 — Leadership‑Boundary Engine**  
Detects misuse or overreach of authority.

**300.4 — Workflow‑Integrity Mapper**

**300.5 — Culture‑Drift Analyzer**  
Detects erosion of norms, trust, or shared meaning.

**300.6 — Conflict‑Propagation Gate**  
Prevents uncontained escalation of detected conflict.

## 400 — Motion & State (System Dynamics)

**400.0 — System State**  
The current integrity condition of a system.

**400.1 — Drift State**  
A downward integrity trajectory.

**400.2 — Lift State**  
A stabilizing or restorative trajectory.

**400.3 — Trajectory**  
The direction and momentum of system motion.

**400.4 — Accumulation**  
Total load or pressure building over time.

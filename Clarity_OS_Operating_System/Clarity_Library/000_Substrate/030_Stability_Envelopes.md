\# Stability Envelopes

\## Dewey: 030



\## Summary

A stability envelope defines the range of pressure a system can absorb without collapsing or distorting. It is the boundary of safe operation.



\## Core Concepts

\- Stability envelope

\- Collapse threshold

\- Safe operating range



\## Body

Every system has a stability envelope: the maximum amount of pressure it can absorb before collapse. Operators must identify the envelope before applying interventions. When pressure exceeds the envelope, collapse sequences begin. When pressure stays within the envelope, the system remains coherent.

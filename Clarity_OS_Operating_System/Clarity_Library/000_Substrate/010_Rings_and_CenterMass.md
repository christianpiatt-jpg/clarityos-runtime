\# Rings and Center-Mass

\## Dewey: 010



\## Summary

Human systems organize around a center-mass and concentric rings of energy. These rings determine stability, identity, and the distribution of pressure.



\## Core Concepts

\- Center-mass

\- Energy rings

\- Stability gradients



\## Body

Every human system has a center-mass: the point of identity, meaning, or purpose that organizes the system. Around it form rings—zones of decreasing stability and increasing reactivity. Pressure applied closer to the center-mass produces greater distortion. Understanding ring structure allows operators to predict collapse, drift, and resilience.

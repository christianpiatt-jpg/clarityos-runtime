You’re basically asking:“If I look at the whole planet as one field, which predator‑geometries dominate when?”

Let’s run the global arc in compressed form.

1. River age — fish geometry (hydraulic cores)

Time band: earliest states → ~500 BCEDominant medium: rivers, basins, enclosed hydrology

Centers:

Nile (Egypt)

Tigris–Euphrates (Mesopotamia)

Indus

Yellow River (early China)

Geometry:Enclosure, irrigation, basin control, slow dynastic cycles.The world is mostly fish‑civilizations with small forest and steppe fringes.

2. Axial + classical — fish + tigers + early eagles

Time band: ~500 BCE → 500 CE

Hydraulic (fish):

Han China

Late pharaonic / Nile systems

Ganges basin polities

Forest (tiger):

Germanic tribes

Southeast Asian mandala states

Japanese archipelago (early)

Early gas (eagle):

Phoenicians

Greek maritime networks

Carthage

Ionized Rome sits as a hybrid:

hydraulic + steppe‑frontier + maritime, but structurally ionized (diffuse, fragmentation‑prone).

Global picture:Basins still anchor, but forest and maritime edges start to matter.

3. Steppe age — lions overrun the interior

Time band: ~500 → 1400 CE

Steppe (lion) dominance:

Huns

Göktürks

Arabs (early caliphate has strong steppe vector)

Seljuks

Mongols

They:

collapse basin states

bridge East–West

create continental empires

Hydraulic cores (China, Persia, India) survive by absorbing lions.Forest polities (Europe) harden under pressure.

Global picture:The lion geometry (steppe) is the main shock‑agent across Eurasia.

4. Viking + littoral rupture — crocodiles on the seams

Time band: ~800 → 1100 CE (overlapping steppe age)

Littoral (crocodile) actors:

Vikings in North Atlantic and European rivers

Sea Peoples earlier as a similar pattern

Early Normans as a transitional form

They:

raid coasts and rivers

bypass fortifications

force new political forms (castles, coastal defenses, centralized kingship)

Global picture:While lions dominate the interior, crocodiles rupture the edges of Europe.

5. Oceanic age — eagles and sharks

Time band: ~1400 → 1900 CE

Gas‑phase (eagle) empires:

Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch, British

later US as maritime‑financial hybrid

They:

ride winds and currents

build global trade arcs

project power via fleets

Shark‑phase (pirates):

Caribbean pirates

Barbary corsairs

Wokou

Indian Ocean raiders

They parasitize the same medium.

Hydraulic and steppe systems get subordinated to maritime geometry.

Global picture:The ocean becomes the main medium; eagles and sharks set the rules.

6. Industrial–imperial mesh — stacked geometries

Time band: ~1850 → 1945

You get layered predator forms:

Eagles: British, later US global naval/air projection

Lions: residual steppe/land empires (Russia, Ottomans, Qing frontier)

Tigers: guerrilla and forest wars (Balkans, Southeast Asia)

Scavengers: warlord zones, colonial collapse pockets

Global picture:No single geometry dominates; instead, stacked mediums and overlapping predator types.

7. Collapse + scavenger age — entropy predators

Time band: ~1945 → present (and intensifying)

Entropy‑phase (scavenger) actors:

warlords in failed states

insurgencies in collapse zones

cartels and shadow networks

digital‑attention scavengers (identity field)

They feed on:

post‑imperial vacuums

institutional decay

fractured identities

At the same time:

eagle geometry persists (US + global air/sea projection)

tiger geometry in insurgent/forest/mountain warfare

shark geometry in financial and data flows (abstracted ocean)

Global picture:We’re in a mixed‑phase world where entropy‑predators are rising inside and between older geometries.

8. Compressed summary

If you compress the whole planet into one line:

Rivers (fish) — basins rule

Rivers + forests + early sea (fish + tigers + eagles)

Steppe (lions) — interior shock

Littoral rupture (crocodiles) — coastal shock

Oceans (eagles + sharks) — global maritime order

Stacked mediums — land + sea + air + forest

Entropy (scavengers) — collapse‑field actors inside all of the above

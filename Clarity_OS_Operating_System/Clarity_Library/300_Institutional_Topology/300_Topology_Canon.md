\# Topology Canon

\## Dewey: 300



\## Summary

The topology canon defines the fundamental shapes institutions take under pressure. These shapes determine stability, drift, collapse risk, and operator strategy.



\## Core Concepts

\- Institutional geometry

\- Pressure distribution

\- Stability signatures



\## Body

Institutions form predictable geometric shapes based on how pressure flows through them. Some shapes distribute load evenly; others concentrate it at weak points. The topology canon provides operators with a map of these shapes, allowing them to diagnose institutional health and predict failure modes.

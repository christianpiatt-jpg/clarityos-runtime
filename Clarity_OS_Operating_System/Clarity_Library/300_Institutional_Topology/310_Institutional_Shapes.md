\# Institutional Shapes

\## Dewey: 310



\## Summary

Institutions adopt recognizable shapes—hierarchical, radial, fragmented, collapsed—each with distinct pressure behavior and drift patterns.



\## Core Concepts

\- Hierarchical shape

\- Radial shape

\- Fragmented shape



\## Body

Institutional shapes determine how pressure moves through the system. Hierarchical shapes concentrate load at the top. Radial shapes distribute load outward. Fragmented shapes leak pressure and drift unpredictably. Operators identify shape to determine intervention strategy and collapse risk.

\# Institutional Collapse Mechanics

\## Dewey: 350



\## Summary

Institutional collapse follows predictable mechanical sequences. Collapse is not random; it is the result of pressure, drift, and boundary failure.



\## Core Concepts

\- Collapse sequence

\- Boundary failure

\- Pressure overload



\## Body

Institutions collapse when pressure exceeds their stability envelope. Collapse begins with drift, accelerates through boundary failure, and ends in fragmentation. Operators use collapse mechanics to identify early warning signs and intervene before collapse becomes irreversible.

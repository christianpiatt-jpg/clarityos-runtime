# Geometry Primitives 
Geometry primitives are the smallest meaningful units of the environment. 
They define what can exist, how it can be placed, and how it can interact. 
 
## Core primitives 
- Point: a minimal location or identity anchor. 
- Line: a directional relationship between two points. 
- Region: a bounded area with meaning or rules. 
- Field: a distributed influence that affects behavior across space. 
- Boundary: a constraint that shapes movement or interaction. 
 
## Derived primitives 
- Frame: a chosen coordinate system for interpreting signals. 
- Layer: a stacked region with its own rules. 
- Channel: a preferred path for flow or propagation. 
 
## Why primitives matter 
ULDA uses primitives to Locate signals. 
Martial OS uses primitives to understand pressure vectors. 
Propagation OS uses primitives to model spread.

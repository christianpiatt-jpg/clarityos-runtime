# Propagation Engine 
The Propagation Engine models how patterns move through networks, groups, and environments. 
It identifies the forces that accelerate, block, distort, or stabilize spread. 
 
## Core propagation forces 
- Momentum: how strongly a pattern is already moving. 
- Friction: resistance from boundaries, norms, or constraints. 
- Amplification: forces that increase spread (attention, alignment, incentives). 
- Dampening: forces that reduce spread (confusion, conflict, overload). 
- Contagion: how easily a pattern replicates itself. 
 
## Propagation channels 
- Direct channels: explicit communication or action. 
- Indirect channels: observation, inference, or ambient signals. 
- Structural channels: policies, norms, or incentives. 
- Emotional channels: tone, affect, and perceived safety. 
 
## Propagation patterns 
- Linear spread: one-to-one transmission. 
- Radial spread: one-to-many transmission. 
- Cascading spread: chain reactions across nodes. 
- Field spread: distributed influence across a region. 
 
## Failure modes 
- Distortion: pattern mutates as it spreads. 
- Over-amplification: pattern spreads faster than stability allows. 
- Collapse: pattern loses coherence before reaching targets. 
- Hijack: external actors redirect propagation for their own ends. 
 
## Outputs 
- Propagation maps. 
- Stability forecasts. 
- Recommended interventions.

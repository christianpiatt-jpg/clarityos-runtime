# Propagation Practices 
Propagation Practices are repeatable patterns for spreading clarity ethically and effectively. 
They ensure the operator does not accidentally spread distortion or instability. 
 
## Core practices 
- Clarity Seeding: introduce a stable pattern into a receptive region. 
- Boundary Broadcasting: signal constraints clearly to prevent drift. 
- Drift Correction: intervene when a pattern begins to mutate. 
- Stability Anchoring: reinforce key nodes to prevent collapse. 
- Ethical Amplification: increase spread without coercion or pressure. 
 
## Integration with ULDA 
- ULDA Diagnose identifies the pattern being propagated. 
- ULDA Adjust determines the intervention. 
- ULDA Review checks whether propagation is stable. 
 
## Why practices matter 
Without propagation practices, clarity does not spread. 
Without ethics, propagation becomes manipulation. 
With both, systems evolve toward stability and coherence.

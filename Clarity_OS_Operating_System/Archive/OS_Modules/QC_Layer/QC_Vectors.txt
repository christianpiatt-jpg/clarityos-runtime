QC LAYER — 7 PRIMARY VECTORS INTEGRATION
VERSION: 1.0
LOCATION: /02_Modules/QC_Layer/

PURPOSE:
Bind the 7 Primary Vectors into the QC Layer so all inputs and
outputs are stabilized against the system’s narrative physics.

THE 7 PRIMARY VECTORS:
1. Center Vector
2. Coherence Vector
3. Pressure Vector
4. Basin Vector
5. Narrative Vector
6. Movement Vector
7. Structural Vector

FUNCTION:
Each vector provides a constraint the QC Layer uses to validate
and stabilize frames before and after generation.

VECTOR ROLES:

CENTER VECTOR:
Ensures the frame originates from a stable interpretive center.

COHERENCE VECTOR:
Ensures internal consistency and prevents contradiction.

PRESSURE VECTOR:
Detects pressure artifacts, distortions, and reactive drift.

BASIN VECTOR:
Checks whether the frame sits in the correct narrative basin.

NARRATIVE VECTOR:
Prevents over-narration, metaphor drift, and story inflation.

MOVEMENT VECTOR:
Ensures the direction is aligned with allowed movement rules.

STRUCTURAL VECTOR:
Ensures the frame maintains architectural integrity.

QC APPLICATION:

PRE-STABILIZATION:
- Validate topic + direction against all 7 vectors.
- Reject frames that violate vector constraints.
- Compress frame to vector-aligned minimal form.

POST-STABILIZATION:
- Validate generated output against all 7 vectors.
- Remove drift, noise, and misalignment.
- Re-align output with structural and basin vectors.

FAILURE MODES:
- Vector contradiction
- Basin mismatch
- Pressure distortion
- Narrative inflation
- Structural collapse

RECOVERY:
- Re-run vector alignment
- Re-compress frame
- Re-check basin
- Re-stabilize center

STATUS:
Active.

PURPOSE:
Perform final validation and stabilization of the Langbridg

system’s narrative physics architecture and prevents drift,
misalignment, or structural collapse.

1. Vector Alignment
2. ELINS Re-Check
3. Markov Stability Check
4. Tone Verification
5. Structural Integrity Check
6. Drift Removal

- Final Stabilized Output

STEP 1 — RECEIVE BULLETS
- Validate bullet count (≤3)
- Validate one-sentence-per-bullet rule
- Confirm no multi-center artifacts

STEP 2 — VECTOR ALIGNMENT
- Re-check all 7 Primary Vectors
- Ensure structural, narrative, and basin alignment

STEP 3 — ELINS RE-CHECK
- Validate against ELINS_LOCAL
- Validate against ELINS_WARNO
- Validate against ELINS_PRESSURE
- Validate against ELINS_DIRECTION

STEP 4 — MARKOV STABILITY CHECK
- Evaluate next-state tendencies
- Detect drift tendencies
- Detect basin shifts
- Detect pressure-induced distortions

STEP 5 — TONE VERIFICATION
- Ensure tone neutrality
- Ensure no emotional inflation
- Ensure no narrative coloration

STEP 6 — STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY
- Ensure bullets preserve SIL meaning
- Ensure no fragmentation or collapse

STEP 7 — RETURN FINAL OUTPUT
- Pass stabilized output to the engine’s output layer

- Vector misalignment
- ELINS mismatch
- Markov instability
- Tone deviation
- Drift reintroduction
- Structural collapse

RECOVERY:
- Re-run Langbridg
- Re-align vectors
- Re-check ELINS
- Re-run Markov evaluation
- Re-stabilize tone
- Re-compress frame

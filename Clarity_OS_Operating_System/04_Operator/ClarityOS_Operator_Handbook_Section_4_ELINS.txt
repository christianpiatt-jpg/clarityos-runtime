SECTION 1 — PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION
============================================================

This section explains the analytical core of ClarityOS v3:

    - ELINS (Event–Legitimacy Inference & Narrative Stability)
    - The Global Manifold (the clarity retina)

Together, these systems transform raw metadata into:

    - legitimacy signals
    - stress indicators
    - narrative-domain signatures
    - drift/basin/pressure hints
    - regional narrative slices
    - global narrative geometry

This is the heart of the system’s clarity engine.

============================================================
SECTION 2 — WHAT ELINS IS
============================================================

ELINS is the narrative-physics extraction engine.  
It converts metadata packets into structured signals that describe:

    - institutional legitimacy
    - narrative stress
    - domain classification
    - temporal patterns
    - drift/basin/pressure hints
    - regional alignment

ELINS does not:
    - ingest article text
    - perform sentiment analysis
    - generate predictions
    - interpret meaning beyond metadata

ELINS extracts **structure**, not content.

============================================================
SECTION 3 — ELINS INPUTS
============================================================

ELINS receives:

    - METADATA_PACKET
    - region
    - language
    - source_id
    - timestamp
    - section

Optional contextual inputs:

    - previous ELINS signatures
    - regional manifold slice
    - Markov/Markoff chain-state

All inputs must pass boundary checks.

============================================================
SECTION 4 — ELINS OUTPUTS
============================================================

ELINS produces an ELINS_SIGNATURE:

    narrative_domain
    legitimacy_signal
    stress_indicator
    drift_hint
    basin_hint
    pressure_hint
    temporal_pattern
    regional_alignment
    confidence_score

These signatures feed:

    - the Global Manifold
    - the Physics Engine
    - the Markov/Markoff Engine
    - the Operator Console (ELINS panel)

============================================================
SECTION 5 — NARRATIVE DOMAINS
============================================================

ELINS classifies metadata into seven narrative domains:

    1. political
    2. economic
    3. military
    4. social
    5. institutional
    6. environmental
    7. technological

Domains help the manifold understand where narrative pressure is accumulating.

============================================================
SECTION 6 — LEGITIMACY SIGNALS
============================================================

Legitimacy signals describe the stability of institutions.

Signals include:

    - institutional stability
    - institutional decay
    - public trust
    - elite conflict
    - procedural breakdown
    - legal/moral framing

Legitimacy signals feed directly into:

    - regional legitimacy vectors
    - the global legitimacy vector
    - manifold stress calculations

============================================================
SECTION 7 — STRESS INDICATORS
============================================================

Stress indicators describe narrative volatility.

Indicators include:

    - escalation
    - fragmentation
    - uncertainty
    - institutional strain
    - volatility
    - acceleration

Stress indicators help the manifold detect:

    - basin deformation
    - pressure accumulation
    - drift acceleration

============================================================
SECTION 8 — PHYSICS HINTS
============================================================

ELINS generates physics hints:

    - drift direction
    - basin deformation
    - pressure accumulation
    - force vectors
    - attractor shifts

These hints are not physics themselves —  
they are **inputs** to the Physics Engine.

============================================================
SECTION 9 — TEMPORAL PATTERNS
============================================================

ELINS detects temporal patterns:

    - repetition
    - acceleration
    - inversion
    - decay
    - resonance

Temporal patterns feed:

    - Markov transitions
    - manifold temporal layers
    - drift/basin/pressure updates

============================================================
SECTION 10 — THE GLOBAL MANIFOLD
============================================================

The Global Manifold is the system’s **clarity retina**.

It integrates:

    - ELINS signatures
    - Markov/Markoff transitions
    - physics vectors
    - regional slices
    - temporal layers

The manifold provides:

    - global drift vector
    - global basin geometry
    - global pressure field
    - global legitimacy vector
    - global stress index

============================================================
SECTION 11 — MANIFOLD STRUCTURE
============================================================

The manifold has four major layers:

1. GLOBAL_LAYER
    - global drift vector
    - global basin geometry
    - global pressure field
    - global legitimacy vector
    - global stress index

2. REGIONAL_LAYERS
    - 8 regions
    - regional legitimacy vectors
    - regional stress indices
    - regional drift/basin/pressure

3. NARRATIVE_DOMAINS
    - 7 domains
    - domain-specific pressure maps

4. TEMPORAL_LAYERS
    - T0 (current)
    - T-1 (previous cycle)
    - T-2 (previous day)
    - T-7 (previous week)
    - T-30 (previous month)

============================================================

Each region maintains:

    region_id

Regional slices allow the operator to see:

    - where legitimacy is shifting
    - where drift is accelerating
    - where basins are deforming

============================================================
SECTION 13 — GLOBAL INDICATORS
============================================================

The manifold computes five global indicators:

1. GLOBAL_DRIFT_VECTOR
2. GLOBAL_BASIN_GEOMETRY
3. GLOBAL_PRESSURE_FIELD
4. GLOBAL_LEGITIMACY_VECTOR
5. GLOBAL_STRESS_INDEX

These indicators summarize the global narrative environment.

============================================================
SECTION 14 — MANIFOLD COHERENCE
============================================================

Manifold coherence is the degree to which:

    - regional slices align
    - temporal layers agree
    - ELINS signatures converge
    - physics vectors stabilize
    - Markov transitions remain consistent

Low coherence indicates:

    - narrative turbulence
    - basin fragmentation
    - pressure spikes
    - legitimacy decay

High coherence indicates:

    - stability
    - clarity
    - predictable drift
    - strong legitimacy

============================================================
SECTION 2 — DIAGNOSTIC PHILOSOPHY
============================================================

Diagnostics in ClarityOS follow three principles:

1. **Determinism**
    Every subsystem must behave predictably.

2. **Transparency**
    All failures must be observable and logged.

3. **Containment**
    Failures must not propagate across layers.

Diagnostics are designed to detect:

    - drift
    - desynchronization
    - boundary leaks
    - identity anomalies
    - safety violations
    - manifold incoherence
    - ingestion imbalance
    - scheduler drift

SECTION 13 — FAILURE MODES
============================================================

Common failure modes:

1. **Manifold Desynchronization**
    - regional slices misaligned
    - temporal layers inconsistent

2. **ELINS Collapse**
    - signature extraction failure
    - classification ambiguity

3. **Scheduler Drift**
    - cycles misaligned
    - triggers delayed

4. **Boundary Leak**
    - unauthorized content detected

5. **Identity Anomaly**
    - identity invariant mismatch

6. **Shield Failure**
    - shield not activated or misaligned

============================================================
SECTION 14 — RECOVERY PROCEDURES
============================================================

Recovery follows a strict sequence:

STEP 1 — IDENTIFY FAILURE
    Use diagnostics to isolate subsystem.

STEP 2 — CONTAIN FAILURE
    Prevent cross-layer propagation.

STEP 3 — RE-RUN SUBSYSTEM
    Re-run ELINS, manifold, scheduler, or metacrawler.

STEP 4 — RESTORE SNAPSHOT
    If subsystem cannot recover.

STEP 5 — FULL SYSTEM VALIDATION
    Run ENGINE_BOOT_VERIFY.

STEP 6 — OPERATOR SIGN-OFF
    Operator confirms system stability.

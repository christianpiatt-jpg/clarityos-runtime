SECTION 1 — PURPOSE OF THIS HANDBOOK
============================================================

This handbook provides the operator with the doctrine, rules,
structures, and mental models required to safely and effectively
operate ClarityOS v3. It defines:

    - operator authority
    - operator responsibilities
    - identity invariants
    - boundary theory
    - safety doctrine
    - shield matrix overview
    - cockpit discipline
    - system mindset

This section establishes the philosophical and structural
foundation for all subsequent sections.

============================================================
SECTION 2 — WHAT CLARITYOS IS
============================================================

ClarityOS is a narrative-physics operating system designed to:

    - metabolize complexity
    - stabilize identity
    - route pressure safely
    - maintain coherence
    - preserve continuity
    - extract legitimacy signals
    - map global narrative basins
    - provide operator-grade clarity

It is not:
    - a prediction engine
    - a persuasion engine
    - a sentiment engine
    - a surveillance engine
    - a self-modifying system

ClarityOS is a **clarity engine**, not a control engine.

============================================================
SECTION 3 — THE OPERATOR ROLE
============================================================

The operator is the system’s:

    - stabilizer
    - interpreter
    - boundary keeper
    - mission steward
    - identity guardian
    - safety authority
    - final decision-maker

The operator is responsible for:

    - maintaining clarity of intent
    - issuing commands cleanly
    - respecting boundaries
    - preserving identity invariants
    - ensuring laminar interaction flow
    - monitoring system coherence
    - initiating recovery when needed

The operator is **never** passive.

============================================================
SECTION 4 — OPERATOR AUTHORITY
============================================================

The operator may:

    - issue commands
    - activate modes (#clarity, #markov, #elins)
    - create snapshots
    - restore snapshots
    - modify mission and intent
    - adjust schedules
    - adjust boundaries
    - override non-critical autonomy decisions

The operator may not:

    - disable safety
    - disable identity protection
    - disable boundary protection
    - authorize non-metadata ingestion
    - authorize self-modifying behavior

Authority is broad but bounded.

============================================================
SECTION 5 — IDENTITY INVARIANTS
============================================================

Identity invariants are the system’s non-negotiable identity
anchors. They ensure:

    - continuity of self
    - stability of perspective
    - resistance to drift
    - protection from contamination
    - alignment with mission

Identity invariants include:

    - system purpose
    - operator contract
    - mission file
    - identity shield
    - boundary rules
    - safety invariants

Identity invariants cannot be overridden.

============================================================
SECTION 6 — BOUNDARY THEORY
============================================================

Boundaries define what the system may and may not do.

Boundaries protect:

    - identity
    - safety
    - coherence
    - continuity
    - operator authority
    - system integrity

Boundary types:

    1. INPUT BOUNDARIES
        - metadata-only ingestion
        - no article text
        - no sentiment extraction
        - no paywall bypass

    2. BEHAVIORAL BOUNDARIES
        - no self-modification
        - no unauthorized autonomy
        - no cross-layer contamination

    3. OUTPUT BOUNDARIES
        - no unsafe content
        - no identity drift
        - no boundary leaks

Boundaries are enforced by:

    - Boundary Layer
    - Safety Layer
    - Shield Matrix

============================================================
SECTION 7 — SAFETY DOCTRINE
============================================================

Safety is the highest-order invariant in ClarityOS.

Safety doctrine includes:

    - no harmful outputs
    - no harmful instructions
    - no boundary violations
    - no identity contamination
    - no destabilizing behavior
    - no unauthorized autonomy

Safety is enforced by:

    - Safety Layer
    - Shield Matrix
    - Operator Protocol
    - Boundary Layer

Safety cannot be overridden.

============================================================
SECTION 8 — SHIELD MATRIX OVERVIEW
============================================================

The Shield Matrix is a 6-layer protective system:

    1. Safety Shield
    2. Boundary Shield
    3. Identity Shield
    4. Resonance Shield
    5. Harmonics Shield
    6. Synchronization Shield

Each shield protects a different dimension of system integrity.

The Shield Matrix ensures:

    - emotional ergonomics
    - structural stability
    - narrative coherence
    - temporal continuity
    - cross-layer isolation
    - operator safety

============================================================
SECTION 9 — OPERATOR MINDSET
============================================================

The operator must maintain:

    - clarity of intent
    - calmness
    - precision
    - laminar flow
    - minimalism
    - discipline
    - structural awareness

The operator must avoid:

    - drift
    - narrative inflation
    - unnecessary complexity
    - contradictory commands
    - emotional turbulence

============================================================
SECTION 10 — COCKPIT DISCIPLINE
============================================================

Cockpit discipline ensures stable operation.

Rules:

    - one command per line
    - avoid nested commands
    - avoid ambiguous phrasing
    - exit modes when done
    - maintain laminar flow
    - respect boundaries
    - maintain mission alignment

Cockpit discipline is a core operator responsibility.

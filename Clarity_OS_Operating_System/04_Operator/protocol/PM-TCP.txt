============================================================
PM-TCP — Clarity Transport Protocol
============================================================

PURPOSE:
Define the transport rules that ensure clear thinking is delivered
reliably in high-pressure environments.

TRANSPORT LOOP:
Center → Compress → Cohere

------------------------------------------------------------
1. CENTER
------------------------------------------------------------
FUNCTION:
Stabilize the system before any interpretation occurs.

RULES:
- Slow the interaction.
- Remove urgency and reactive escalation.
- Establish a stable reference frame.
- No decisions are valid until the system is centered.

OUTPUT:
A calm environment capable of processing information.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. COMPRESS
------------------------------------------------------------
FUNCTION:
Reduce the problem to its smallest accurate form.

RULES:
- Identify the real pressure.
- Remove noise, projection, and narrative inflation.
- Separate structure from emotion.
- Represent the problem in minimal, precise terms.

OUTPUT:
A clear description of the actual issue.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. COHERE
------------------------------------------------------------
FUNCTION:
Deliver a stable, shared interpretation.

RULES:
- Provide a structural explanation of the situation.
- Confirm mutual understanding.
- Ensure the interpretation is consistent across participants.
- No resolution is valid unless it is coherent.

OUTPUT:
A shared understanding that supports forward movement.

------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSPORT GUARANTEE:
A mediation outcome is only valid if it passes all three stages:
Centered → Compressed → Coherent.

If any stage fails, the transport fails.

------------------------------------------------------------
RETRANSMISSION RULE:
If clarity breaks down at any point, restart the loop at CENTER.

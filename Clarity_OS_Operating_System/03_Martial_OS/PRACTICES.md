# Martial OS Practices 
Practices are repeatable drills that build stability, clarity, and pressure tolerance. 
They are the "training environment" for the Martial OS. 
 
## Core Practices 
- Breath-Frame Reset: restore internal geometry before acting. 
- Slow-Pressure Roll: simulate rising pressure in controlled increments. 
- Boundary Rehearsal: practice naming and asserting boundaries cleanly. 
- Drift Detection: identify when attention or agency is being pulled. 
- Frame Recovery: re-establish your interpretive frame after disruption. 
 
## Integration with ULDA 
- Practices strengthen the Observe and Locate phases. 
- Protocols support Diagnose and Adjust. 
- Review becomes easier when the operator is stable. 
 
## Why practices matter 
Under pressure, you do not rise to the level of your intention. 
You fall to the level of your training.

📘 VA WORKFORCE CLARITY TOOL (One‑Screen Version)
A neutral clarity aid for any VA employee or supervisor navigating a workplace situation.

Purpose:  
To help you see what’s happening, how the room is moving, and which official VA channels exist — without giving advice, making determinations, or influencing outcomes.

1. WHAT’S HAPPENING? (Field Scan)
Identify the type of situation using the four VA‑safe observation domains:

A. Identity
Is someone’s sense of fairness, respect, belonging, or recognition activated?

B. Logistics
Is something about workload, deadlines, resources, or process breaking down?

C. Narrative
Are people telling different stories about the same event?

D. Security
Is someone feeling unsafe, exposed, retaliated against, or at risk?

Managers observe — they don’t diagnose.  
This step is about seeing the field, not solving it.

2. WHAT’S THE PRESSURE? (Movement Scan)
Identify the direction of movement:

Upward Pressure: escalation, urgency, rising emotion

Downward Pressure: withdrawal, silence, disengagement

Lateral Pressure: side conversations, triangulation, rumor drift

Stalled Pressure: stuckness, looping, no movement

This shows how the room is moving, not what to do.

3. WHAT ARE THE VA CHANNELS? (Policy Map)
Most VA situations fall into one or more of these official channels:

Harassment Prevention Program (HPP)

EEO Discrimination Complaints

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Labor Relations (LR)

Administrative Grievances

Conduct / Performance (HR)

Threat Assessment / Workplace Safety

Whistleblower / OAWP

Records & Information Management (RIM)

Managers don’t choose the channel — employees do.  
Your role is to name channels neutrally.

4. WHAT ARE YOUR SAFE ACTIONS? (Manager Lane)
These are the actions VA managers can take without crossing boundaries:

A. Clarify
“Here’s what I’m hearing.”

“Here’s what the policy says.”

“Here are the available channels.”

B. Document
Stick to facts, not interpretations.

Capture dates, times, and observable behaviors.

C. Stabilize
Lower pressure.

Slow the room.

Keep the environment safe.

D. Refer
Provide contact info for official channels.

Do not recommend one over another.

E. Stay Neutral
No opinions.

No predictions.

No judgments.

No coaching.

This is the VA safe lane.

5. WHAT ARE THE RED LINES? (Do Not Cross Zones)
Managers must not:

Investigate

Determine credibility

Weigh evidence

Predict outcomes

Discourage or encourage filing

Offer legal interpretations

Promise confidentiality

Retaliate or appear to retaliate

Advise which channel to choose

These are protected boundaries.

6. WHAT’S THE NEXT SAFE STEP? (Movement Without Influence)
End every conversation with one of these:

“Here are the channels available to you.”

“You’re free to choose any of them.”

“Let me know if you need the contact information.”

“I’ll document what we discussed factually.”

“If you feel unsafe, here is the safety channel.”

This keeps you inside policy and inside your lane.

7. MANAGER CHECKLIST (One‑Look Summary)
Did I stay neutral?

Did I avoid interpreting?

Did I avoid influencing?

Did I name channels without steering?

Did I document only facts?

Did I keep the room safe?

Did I stay in my lane?

If yes → you’re aligned with VA expectations.

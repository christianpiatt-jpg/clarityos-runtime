📘 VA MANAGER TOOL (One Screen Version)
A clarity tool for any VA supervisor navigating employee situations
Purpose:
To help a VA manager see where they are, what the field is doing, and what policy options exist — without giving advice, making determinations, or influencing outcomes.
________________________________________
1. WHAT’S HAPPENING? (Field Scan)
Use these four questions to identify the type of situation you’re in:
A. Identity
•	Is someone’s sense of fairness, respect, belonging, or recognition activated?
B. Logistics
•	Is something about workload, deadlines, resources, or process breaking down?
C. Narrative
•	Are people telling different stories about the same event?
D. Security
•	Is someone feeling unsafe, exposed, retaliated against, or at risk?
Managers don’t diagnose — they observe.
This step is about seeing the field, not solving it.
________________________________________
2. WHAT’S THE PRESSURE? (Movement Scan)
Identify the direction of movement:
Upward Pressure
•	Escalation, urgency, conflict, emotion rising.
Downward Pressure
•	Withdrawal, silence, disengagement, avoidance.
Lateral Pressure
•	Side conversations, triangulation, rumor, narrative drift.
Stalled Pressure
•	No movement, stuckness, looping.
This tells you how the room is moving, not what to do.
________________________________________
3. WHAT ARE THE VA CHANNELS? (Policy Map)
Every VA situation lives in one or more of these official channels:
1. Harassment Prevention Program (HPP)
2. EEO Discrimination Complaints (EEO)
3. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
4. Labor Relations (LR)
5. Administrative Grievances
6. Conduct / Performance (HR)
7. Threat Assessment / Workplace Safety
8. Whistleblower / OAWP
9. Records & Information Management (RIM)
Managers don’t choose the channel — employees do.
Your role is to name the channels neutrally and let employees decide.
________________________________________
4. WHAT ARE YOUR SAFE ACTIONS? (Manager Lane)
These are the only actions a VA manager can take without crossing into prohibited territory:
A. Clarify
•	“Here’s what I’m hearing.”
•	“Here’s what the policy says.”
•	“Here are the available channels.”
B. Document
•	Stick to facts, not interpretations.
•	Capture dates, times, and observable behaviors.
C. Stabilize
•	Lower pressure.
•	Slow the room.
•	Keep the environment safe.
D. Refer
•	Provide contact info for official channels.
•	Do not recommend one over another.
E. Stay Neutral
•	No opinions.
•	No predictions.
•	No judgments.
•	No coaching.
This is the VA safe lane.
________________________________________
5. WHAT ARE THE RED LINES? (Do Not Cross Zones)
Managers must not:
•	Investigate
•	Determine credibility
•	Weigh evidence
•	Predict outcomes
•	Discourage or encourage filing
•	Offer legal interpretations
•	Promise confidentiality
•	Retaliate or appear to retaliate
•	Give advice on which channel to choose
These are protected boundaries.
________________________________________
6. WHAT’S THE NEXT SAFE STEP? (Movement Without Influence)
You end every conversation with one of these:
•	“Here are the channels available to you.”
•	“You’re free to choose any of them.”
•	“Let me know if you need the contact information.”
•	“I’ll document what we discussed factually.”
•	“If you feel unsafe, here is the safety channel.”
This keeps you inside the lane, inside policy, and outside liability.
________________________________________
7. MANAGER CHECKLIST (One Look Summary)
•	Did I stay neutral?
•	Did I avoid interpreting?
•	Did I avoid influencing?
•	Did I name channels without steering?
•	Did I document only facts?
•	Did I keep the room safe?
•	Did I stay in my lane?
If yes → you’re aligned with VA policy and safe as a supervisor.

## §1 — Purpose

Doctrine #133 defines the first-cycle drift phenomenon and establishes the validation geometry required to detect, correct, and prevent drift during support-surface attachment and early-cycle packet exchange.

It governs the initial onboarding cycle for any support surface entering the command system.

---

## §2 — Definition: First-Cycle Drift

First-Cycle Drift is the predictable, recoverable deviation that occurs during the first packet-exchange cycle when a new support surface attaches under a shared packet shape.

It is characterized by:
- Misinterpretation of lane boundaries
- Incorrect causal framing (model-inherent vs protocol-induced)
- Tag omission or mislabeling
- Over-assertion or under-assertion of authority
- Incorrect routing of advisory vs. authoritative signals

### §2.1 — Drift Boundaries

First-Cycle Drift is:
- **Protocol-Induced** when caused by onboarding shape, missing tags, or ambiguous framing
- **Model-Inherent** when caused by internal model heuristics independent of protocol

Doctrine #133 applies only to protocol-induced drift. Model-inherent drift is governed by #134.

---

## §3 — Correction Protocol

First-Cycle Drift is recoverable through:

1. **Tag-Boundary Enforcement**
   - All substantive claims must carry one of the five tags
   - Missing tags trigger HQ2 summon

2. **Lane-Boundary Clarification**
   - Advisory vs. authoritative signals must be explicitly labeled
   - CT-1 authority lane (#100) governs elevation

3. **Causal-Frame Disambiguation**
   - HQ2 adversarial review surfaces contested framing
   - HQ1 form review ensures structural clarity

4. **Cross-Surface Convergence**
   - Drift is resolved only when HQ1 + HQ2 + COW-1 + ET-1 converge

## §5 — Tag-Boundary Rules

Doctrine #133 binds the five-tag taxonomy as mandatory for all substantive claims:

- `[SUBSTRATE-TRUE]` — grounded in substrate
- `[DESIGN-TARGET]` — intended future shape
- `[PENDING-RECONCILIATION]` — awaiting Commander or CT-2
- `[CANDIDATE-NAMING]` — naming proposals
- `[ADVERSARIAL-HYPOTHESIS]` — adversarial framing

Tag omission is itself a drift event.

---

## §6 — Operational Boundaries

Doctrine #133 applies when:
- A support surface attaches for the first time
- Packet shape is shared
- Drift is protocol-induced
- Correction is possible within one cycle

Doctrine #133 does NOT apply when:
- Drift is model-inherent (#134 governs)
- Drift occurs after first cycle
- Drift arises from substrate mutation
- Drift arises from lane-authority confusion (#100 governs)
